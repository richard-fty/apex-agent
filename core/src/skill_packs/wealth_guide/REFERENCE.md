# Wealth Guide Reference

## Situation classes

- `cash_heavy`: user has meaningful idle cash relative to investable assets
- `rsu_concentrated`: employer equity is too large a slice of net worth
- `home_saving`: home purchase is a near-term stated goal
- `long_term_builder`: long horizon, no acute concentration or debt issue
- `debt_burdened`: high-interest debt is large enough to dominate allocation

## Flag meanings

- `high_interest_debt`: debt with rate around 7%+ or credit-card style debt
- `concentration_risk`: one risky asset is too large a share of wealth
- `cash_drag`: liquid cash is too high for the stated horizon
- `housing_goal`: home purchase is part of the goal set
- `low_emergency_buffer`: cash covers fewer than 6 months of expenses, only when expenses are known

## Path vocabulary

- `T-bills`: low-risk, high-liquidity parking for near-term capital
- `HYSA-only`: keep flexibility when the user values safety and simplicity
- `split`: divide cash between safety and long-term investing
- `index`: broad diversified long-term investing path
- `mixed-stocks`: long-term path with some active stock risk
- `debt-first`: use excess cash to eliminate expensive debt first
- `diversify`: reduce employer-stock or single-asset concentration gradually
- `hold-with-hedge`: keep some concentrated exposure while reducing risk

## Concept priorities

Use only the concepts required by the paths being compared.

Common concept set:
- liquidity risk
- time horizon
- concentration risk
- opportunity cost
- sequence-of-returns risk
- debt interest as a guaranteed drag

## Tone

- calm
- non-hype
- educational
- tradeoff-oriented

## Minimum-input mode

Default to a first-pass strategy when the user shares income, cash, and a simple goal.
Do not ask for a full financial profile before producing useful paths.
Treat missing expenses, debt, and account balances as assumptions the user can refine later.
